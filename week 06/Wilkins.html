(W)rapped Space:

The Architecture of Hip Hop

CRAIG L. WILKINS, University of Minnesota


Craig L. Wilkins postulates based on the concept of meaning and space being reciprocal that culture can be studied and utilized to create communal gathering spaces based on culture in order to uplift and celebrate rather than be oppressed and stifled. Specifically in his essay he focuses on black culture, rap and hip hop, and the result a “Hip Hop Park” can have on the community around it as a result of community interaction and engagement.
